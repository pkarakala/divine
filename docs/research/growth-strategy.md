# Divine — Growth & Go-to-Market Strategy

## Core Insight

Tinder launched by going **sorority → fraternity** on college campuses. Divine already HAS the sorority/fraternity infrastructure. We don't need to build community — we need to activate it.

---

## Launch Playbook

### Phase 0: Pre-Launch (4-6 weeks before app goes live)

#### 1. Build the Waitlist
- Landing page: "Apply for Early Access to Divine"
- Collect: name, email, D9 organization, chapter, city
- Display live waitlist counter (social proof)
- "Refer a line sister/brother to move up the list"

#### 2. Recruit Chapter Ambassadors
- 2-3 ambassadors per organization in launch city (Atlanta)
- Total: 18-27 ambassadors across all 9 orgs
- Compensation: lifetime Divine Elite subscription + referral bonuses
- Role: share in chapter groupchats, post on social, attend launch events

#### 3. Create Buzz
- Instagram/TikTok content: "D9 dating culture" content (memes, stories, aspirational content)
- Teaser videos: "Something exclusive is coming for the Divine 9"
- Greek influencer partnerships (D9 content creators, stroll accounts)
- Countdown on social media

#### 4. Women-First Strategy
- Open waitlist to sorority members 2 weeks before fraternity members
- When app launches: sorority members get 48-hour head start
- Ensures women are already active when men arrive
- Prevents the 73% male ratio that killed Tinder's UX

## Launch Strategy (Atlanta First)

### Why Atlanta
1. Largest concentration of D9 members outside D.C.
2. Multiple HBCUs (Morehouse, Spelman, Clark Atlanta)
3. Young professional Black community is massive
4. Event-heavy culture (perfect for activation)
5. D9 culture is extremely visible and celebrated

### Week 1: Soft Launch
- Invite-only: first 500 women, then first 500 men
- Personal invites from ambassadors
- In-app onboarding feedback prompts
- Daily monitoring of gender ratio, match rates, messaging

### Week 2-4: Open Launch (Atlanta)
- Open registration for all verified D9 members in Atlanta
- Launch event: Divine Mixer (in-person event, app required for entry)
- PR push: local Black media, D9 community blogs, Watch The Yard
- Social content from launch event

### Success Trigger to Expand
- 500+ weekly active users
- Gender ratio 40-60% women
- 50+ matches/week
- Positive sentiment (app store reviews, social mentions)

## Organic Growth Channels (70% of acquisition)

### 1. Chapter Network Effect
- Once 3-5 members of a chapter join, the rest follow (FOMO within tight groups)
- Line sisters/brothers tell each other
- Chapter groupchats (GroupMe, iMessage) are the primary distribution channel
- "Who's on Divine?" becomes chapter conversation

### 2. Event-Based Growth
- **Convention season** (summer): Presence at Boules, Grand Chapter meetings
  - Delta Sigma Theta Grand Chapter: 40,000+ attendees
  - AKA Boule: 25,000+ attendees
  - Kappa Conclave: 15,000+ attendees
- **Homecoming** (fall): Activations at HBCU homecomings
- **Greek picnics** (spring/summer): Branded presence at regional events
- **Step shows**: Sponsorship/presence at major step events

### 3. Success Stories
- Every couple that forms on Divine = organic marketing
- In-app prompt: "Met on Divine? Share your story!"
- Social media amplification of success stories
- "Divine Couples" Instagram highlights

### 4. Content Marketing
- Instagram/TikTok: D9 dating culture content
  - "What it's like dating a Kappa/Delta/Alpha..."
  - Greek love stories
  - D9 date ideas
  - Stroll/step show proposal videos
- YouTube: longer-form content on D9 relationships
- Blog: SEO for "D9 dating", "Greek dating app"

### 5. Referral Program
- Every member gets 3 invite codes
- Successful referral = 1 free week of Divine+
- Referred member skips waitlist
- Leaderboard: top referrers get recognition

## Paid Growth Channels (30% of acquisition)

### Social Media Ads
- **Instagram** (primary): targeted at D9 hashtags, HBCU followers, Greek life interests
- **TikTok**: short-form video ads in D9 content feeds
- **Facebook**: targeted at D9 groups and alumni networks
- Budget: $5K/month Year 1, scaling with revenue

### Influencer Partnerships
- D9 content creators (Watch The Yard, Greek life TikTokers)
- HBCU influencers
- Cost: $500-$2,000 per creator post
- Focus on authentic users who are actually D9 members

### Event Sponsorship
- Co-sponsor chapter events: $500-$2,000 per event
- National convention presence: $5,000-$15,000 per convention
- HBCU homecoming activations: $2,000-$5,000 per school

## Retention Strategy

### Daily Engagement Loops
- **Most Compatible**: one daily algorithmic pick → creates reason to return
- **Limited likes** (8/day free): reset at 4 AM → daily return cadence
- **"Your Turn"** indicators: nudge conversations forward
- **New likes notification**: push when someone likes your profile

### Weekly Engagement
- **Weekly Standouts**: curated profiles worth a rose
- **Event listings**: upcoming D9 events in your city
- **Profile tips**: "Add a voice prompt to get 3x more likes"

### Monthly Engagement
- **We Met follow-ups**: "Did you meet [match]? How'd it go?"
- **Profile refresh reminders**: "Update your photos for cuffing season"
- **Success stories**: monthly email/notification of Divine couples

### Churn Prevention
- **Pause instead of delete**: "Take a break" feature preserves profile
- **Win-back push**: "3 people liked you while you were away"
- **Seasonal reactivation**: Homecoming, Valentine's Day, cuffing season pushes

## Competitive Moat Building

### Why Competitors Can't Easily Replicate

1. **Cultural authenticity** — a non-D9 company building this would face community skepticism
2. **Chapter-level network effects** — once chapters are on Divine, switching costs are high
3. **Verification relationships** — building trust with national org leadership takes time
4. **First-mover data** — matching algorithm trained on D9-specific preferences improves over time
5. **Event infrastructure** — relationships with event organizers, venues, conventions compound
6. **Brand equity** — "Divine" becomes synonymous with "D9 dating" in the community vocabulary
